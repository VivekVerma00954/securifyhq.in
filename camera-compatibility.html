These brands intentionally lock their cameras to their own apps and cloud platforms. If your property currently uses these cameras, we'll recommend an upgrade path during your consultation.

🔌 What Makes a Camera Compatible?

In simple terms: if a camera supports RTSP or ONVIF, we can almost certainly work with it. Here's what that means.

  • 📡
    RTSP (Real Time Streaming Protocol) — The industry-standard protocol for streaming live video from IP cameras. Most professional and semi-professional cameras support it. It's what lets our monitoring platform receive your camera feed securely.
  • 🔗
    ONVIF Compliance — An interoperability standard that most commercial cameras follow. ONVIF-compliant cameras typically include RTSP support and are designed for professional integration.
  • 📼
    DVR/NVR with RTSP channel streams — If your property has older analog/coaxial cameras connected to a DVR (common in India), you may not need to replace anything. Most professional DVRs from Hikvision, Dahua, and CP Plus expose an RTSP stream for each connected camera channel — effectively bridging analog cameras into our monitoring platform. We connect to the DVR's channel streams the same way we would individual IP cameras.
  • 🌐
    Static or DDNS-accessible local IP — Your cameras need a consistent local IP address so our system can always find them. We configure this as part of the setup process.
  • 🔒
    Secure remote access — This is handled by the networking hardware we install. You don't need to open ports on your router or expose cameras to the internet directly — our setup method is more secure than that.

📌 Important: Upload Speed Is What Matters

Most internet plans advertise download speed, but for camera monitoring you need upload speed — because your cameras send video out. Jio Fiber and Airtel Fiber plans are symmetric or near-symmetric, meaning upload speeds are similar to download. We'll test your connection during setup and flag any issues before going live.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about compatibility, setup, and costs.

Do I need to buy new cameras for SecurifyHQ?
Not necessarily. SecurifyHQ works with most standard IP cameras that support RTSP — the industry-standard protocol used by Hikvision, Dahua, CP Plus, Reolink, TP-Link Tapo, and many others. If you already have compatible cameras installed, we can integrate them directly. We'll confirm compatibility during your initial consultation.
Why doesn't SecurifyHQ work with Ring or Google Nest cameras?
Ring, Google Nest, Amazon Blink, and similar cloud-only cameras are designed to work exclusively within their own closed ecosystems. They do not support RTSP or any open streaming protocol, which means third-party professional monitoring platforms cannot connect to them. To use SecurifyHQ, you need cameras with RTSP support.
What internet speed do I need?
We recommend approximately 2–3 Mbps upload per camera for reliable monitoring. For a 4-camera setup, aim for at least 10 Mbps upload. Most Jio Fiber and Airtel Fiber plans in India comfortably meet this. We'll review your connection during setup and advise on any adjustments needed before going live.
What does the setup fee cover?
The setup fee covers the professional networking hardware we install at your property, full system configuration, camera integration, encrypted remote access setup, end-to-end testing, and handover documentation. The hardware — which forms the bulk of the cost — belongs to you and stays at your property permanently.
Can I use wireless cameras?
Yes, provided the camera supports RTSP streaming. Many wireless cameras from Reolink, TP-Link Tapo, and other brands include RTSP support. However, for critical security monitoring we generally recommend at least some wired cameras for reliability. We'll advise on the right mix for your property.
What if my cameras aren't listed here?
Our compatibility list covers the most common brands but isn't exhaustive. If your camera brand or model isn't listed, send us the details and we'll check. The key question is simply: does it support RTSP? If yes, there's a very high chance we can integrate it.
How long does the setup take?
A typical setup takes half a day to a full day, depending on camera count and property layout. We coordinate with you (or your local contact) in India to arrange the installation visit. We do the technical work — you don't need to be there in person if you're overseas.
