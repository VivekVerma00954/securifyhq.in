Punjab Property Security for NRIs: Jalandhar, Amritsar, Ludhiana, Mohali and Beyond

Punjab has the highest concentration of NRI property owners in India. It also has some of the most specific challenges for remote property management. This guide covers what actually matters, city by city.

By SecurifyHQ · March 2026 · 11 min read

Punjab sends more people abroad than any other Indian state. The Punjabi diaspora is concentrated in the UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and the US — and most of them have family property back home. That property sits in Jalandhar, Amritsar, Ludhiana, Mohali, Patiala, or one of hundreds of smaller towns and villages across the state.

Managing that property from abroad presents challenges that are specific to Punjab — not just general "NRI property" challenges. This guide is for NRIs with Punjab properties specifically.

Why Punjab Is Different for NRI Property Security

There are several factors that make Punjab properties a distinct security case:

The NRI property signal effect

Properties owned by NRIs are often visibly different from those owned by local residents — larger, better-built, sometimes containing imported goods or electronics. More importantly, they're often known to be unoccupied for extended periods. In tight-knit Punjab communities, everyone knows when a family has gone abroad. A visibly vacant NRI property is a signal to opportunistic actors.

Caretaker dependency

Most NRI Punjab property owners depend heavily on a single trusted contact — typically a relative — for property oversight. This creates a single point of failure. When that relative gets busy, relocates, or passes away, the entire oversight structure collapses. Professional monitoring provides a layer that doesn't depend on any one person's availability.

Power infrastructure

Despite improvements, power cuts remain a reality across Punjab — particularly in smaller cities and rural areas during summer months. A camera system without UPS backup can go dark for hours. This is not a minor inconvenience; it's a reliable blind spot that experienced property criminals know to exploit.

Encroachment risk

Punjab has seen a significant number of property encroachment cases involving NRI-owned land — particularly agricultural plots, vacant plots, and properties with absent owners. Extended absence makes encroachment easier; camera coverage and documented visits create a deterrent and an evidence trail.

SecurifyHQ's monitoring centre is based in Punjab. Our operators understand local context — Punjab dialects, local neighbourhood dynamics, seasonal patterns, and what "normal" activity looks like vs. what doesn't. That local knowledge makes a difference in how alerts are interpreted.

Jalandhar: The NRI Capital

📍 Jalandhar Highest NRI Density

Jalandhar — particularly areas like Model Town, Guru Nanak Pura, New Jawahar Nagar, and surroundings — has the highest concentration of NRI-owned properties in Punjab. The term "NRI colony" is used informally for entire neighbourhoods where most residents live abroad.

  • Common property types: Large standalone houses (kothi), multi-storey residential builds, plot investments
  • Primary security concerns: Opportunistic theft from vacant properties, caretaker-related incidents, encroachment on large plots
  • Infrastructure: Relatively good fibre internet availability in established residential areas; UPS still recommended
  • Local monitoring advantage: SecurifyHQ operates from the Jalandhar area — our operators know the city's areas, local dynamics, and can coordinate with trusted contacts effectively

The density of NRI properties in Jalandhar is both a reassurance (established communities, local services catering to NRIs) and a vulnerability (concentrated known-vacant properties). The most important thing for a Jalandhar NRI property owner is consistent monitoring — not just cameras, but someone watching them.

Amritsar: Heritage City, Modern Security Needs

📍 Amritsar Heritage + Tourism

Amritsar presents a different security picture from Jalandhar. The city's tourism industry, the Golden Temple complex, and busy commerce create a more dynamic environment. NRI properties here are often in areas adjacent to high-traffic zones.

  • Common property types: Heritage havelis and older properties, residential areas in Ranjit Avenue, Green Avenue, Majitha Road
  • Primary security concerns: Higher general foot traffic around certain areas, older property infrastructure (weaker physical security), seasonal tourist-period pressures
  • Specific risk: Heritage properties are particularly vulnerable — older locks, structural features that make access easier, and sometimes contents of historical value
  • Festival impact: Gurpurabs and major Sikh festival seasons see massive foot traffic surges around the Golden Temple area and beyond

Camera placement for Amritsar properties

For properties in areas with higher ambient foot traffic, camera placement matters more. You want clear identification-quality footage of faces at approach/entry points, not just motion-triggered wide shots. Discuss specific placement with a professional installer familiar with your area.

Ludhiana: Industrial City, Different Risk Profile

📍 Ludhiana Industrial Hub

Ludhiana's character as Punjab's industrial capital means a different NRI property profile. Properties here are often investment properties — rented to migrant workers or business tenants — rather than family homes.

  • Common property types: Apartments, commercial-residential mixed use, investment flats in areas like BRS Nagar, Pakhowal Road, Civil Lines
  • Primary security concerns: Tenant management at distance, property condition oversight, rental disputes
  • Infrastructure: Generally good urban infrastructure; fibre widely available
  • Specific challenge: High population density and transient tenants make verification of who is actually in your property more important

For Ludhiana NRI property owners with tenanted properties, the monitoring focus shifts slightly: cameras for perimeter and common areas (lobby, parking), with professional monitoring providing regular status confirmations and responding to any unusual access patterns.

Mohali and Chandigarh: Tricity Considerations

📍 Mohali / Chandigarh / Panchkula Tricity

The Chandigarh Tricity area is increasingly attractive for NRI investment — planned infrastructure, better connectivity, proximity to Chandigarh International Airport. Properties in Mohali's sectors, Aerocity, or Kharar are growing in NRI ownership.

  • Common property types: Apartments in gated societies, plotted developments, builder floors
  • Primary security concerns: Gated society cameras don't replace property-level monitoring; common areas monitored but individual units are not
  • Specific consideration: Many NRI Tricity property owners assume the society's security is sufficient — it is not, for a property that may be vacant for months
  • Infrastructure: Among the best in Punjab — reliable power, good fibre availability, modern building infrastructure

Common Security Threats Across Punjab

Across all Punjab cities and towns, NRI property owners face a consistent set of threats:

1. Opportunistic theft from vacant properties

The most common incident type — someone taking advantage of known vacancy to steal fixtures, cables, building materials, or items left in the property. These incidents often happen incrementally: a small theft, then another, each one testing whether anyone is watching. Consistent monitoring visibility is the primary deterrent.

2. Utility theft

Water connections and electricity connections are particularly vulnerable when an NRI property is vacant. Unauthorised tapping of water connections from vacant properties is a known issue in some Punjab areas. Camera coverage of utility entry points combined with periodic physical checks catches this early.

3. Encroachment

Agricultural land and vacant plots owned by NRIs have seen encroachment — both by neighbours expanding gradually and, in some documented cases, fraudulent sale by family members or associates with access to documents. Camera coverage of land boundaries and registered PoA with a trusted lawyer are the primary protections.

4. Caretaker misuse

Extended-absence caretaker arrangements can lead to the property being used in ways not authorised — hosting family, subletting rooms, using utilities. Cameras provide objective verification that this isn't happening; professional monitoring picks it up even when you're not actively watching.

Recommended Security Setup for Punjab Properties

Based on the specific dynamics of Punjab NRI properties, here's a recommended baseline setup:

  • Cameras: Minimum 4 — main gate, front entrance, rear access point, and one overview of the property from a high position. Night vision is essential. Use Hikvision or Dahua for quality; CP Plus for budget-conscious setups.
  • UPS: Non-negotiable for Punjab. Minimum 4-hour backup for camera NVR and networking equipment. Larger is better.
  • Internet: Fibre connection (BSNL, Airtel, Jio Fiber) preferred over mobile hotspot, which is unreliable for consistent monitoring. A mobile backup SIM in a secondary router provides failover.
  • Professional monitoring: India-based operators monitoring during Indian hours, with structured escalation to your local contacts.
  • Local contacts: At minimum, a primary contact and a backup — not just one person. Ensure both contacts are briefed on how the monitoring system works and what to do when called.

Festival Season in Punjab: A Special Case

Punjab's festival calendar is significant — Diwali, Lohri, Baisakhi, Gurpurabs, and wedding season (Nov–Feb) all represent periods of heightened activity. For NRI properties, this creates two conflicting dynamics:

Positive: More people around, more social activity, more natural surveillance from active neighbourhood presence.

Negative: More movement, more strangers, more noise that masks incident activity. Monitoring alert volumes spike significantly during these periods. Alert fatigue (ignoring alerts because there are so many) becomes a real risk for self-monitoring NRIs.

⚠ Festival season is when many caretaker check-ins become irregular. Your caretaker is busy with their own family celebrations. This is precisely when professional monitoring fills the gap — operators are working their regular schedule regardless of the festival calendar.
